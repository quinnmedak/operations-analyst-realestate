## Current State — KPI Row

Four headline numbers that give an immediate read on where the commercial real estate market stands.

---

### Office REIT Avg Price

**What it is:** The average stock price across the three office REITs in the dashboard — Boston Properties (BXP), SL Green (SLG), and Vornado (VNO). A REIT (Real Estate Investment Trust) is a publicly traded company that owns office buildings. Its stock price reflects what investors collectively think those buildings are worth.

**Why it matters:** Public REIT prices move faster than private real estate transactions, which can take months to close. When office REIT prices fall, it signals that investors have lost confidence in office properties — which is an early warning for the broader office market. When prices recover, it signals returning confidence before actual deal volume picks up.

---

### Industrial REIT Avg Price

**What it is:** The average stock price across the two industrial REITs — Prologis (PLD) and STAG Industrial (STAG). These companies own warehouses, distribution centers, and logistics facilities.

**Why it matters:** Industrial REIT prices staying elevated while office prices fell is the central story of this dashboard. It shows that investors have continued to back the warehouse and logistics sector even as they pulled out of office — which reflects real differences in underlying demand.

---

### Fed Funds Rate

**What it is:** The interest rate set by the Federal Reserve — the baseline cost of borrowing money in the U.S. economy. Shown as the quarterly average from FRED (Federal Reserve Economic Data).

**Why it matters:** Commercial real estate is heavily debt-financed. When the Fed raises rates, borrowing gets more expensive, which makes real estate less attractive as an investment and pushes valuations down. When rates fall, the reverse happens. This single number explains most of the office market's decline from 2022–2024 and most of the recovery beginning in 2025. It is the most important external driver of CRE valuations.

---

### CRE Loan Delinquency Rate

**What it is:** The percentage of commercial real estate loans that are past due or in default. Sourced from FRED (series: DRCRELEXFACBS). Shown as the most recent quarter with available data — this series lags by one to two quarters.

**Why it matters:** Rising delinquency means property owners are struggling to service their debt — often because rents fell, vacancy rose, or they borrowed at low rates and can't refinance at today's higher rates. It is a measure of financial stress in the system. When delinquency rises while deal volume is also recovering (as in 2025), it reflects two things happening simultaneously: distressed legacy loans working through the system, and new buyers coming in at reset prices.
