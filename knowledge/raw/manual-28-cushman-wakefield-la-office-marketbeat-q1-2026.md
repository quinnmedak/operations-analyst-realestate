## Economic Overview

LA County employment increased 34,200 nonfarm jobs (+0.8% YOY, Jan 2025–Jan 2026) — growth concentrated in healthcare and education (+39,200 jobs), not office-using sectors. Professional and business services added only 5,000 jobs (+0.8% YOY). Government (-12,100), financial activities (-1,200), and construction (-3,900) all contracted YOY. Unemployment improved to 5.5% (down from 5.8% YOY). Projected employment growth of 0.7% in 2026 and 0.5% in 2027 — both below the five-year historical average of 1.5%.

---

## Supply and Demand

Vacancy rose to 23.6%, up 60 bps QOQ and 20 bps YOY. Net absorption was -1.1M SF — move-outs and space givebacks continued to outpace occupancy gains. Leasing totaled 2.2M SF, down 20.1% QOQ and 23.6% YOY. Renewal leasing especially soft at 649,631 SF, down 65.4% QOQ.

Notable Q1 2026 leases:
- North LA County Regional Center: 146,515 SF expansion, West Valley Corporate Center (Chatsworth)
- On Location (event planning): 108,272 SF, Union Bank Plaza (Downtown LA)
- Faraday: 99,600 SF, El Segundo (South Bay)

Most active industries: financial (252,721 SF), government (232,250 SF), professional services (221,347 SF), legal (194,723 SF)

---

## Capital Markets

Bank of America Plaza (333 S. Hope St., Bunker Hill, 1.42M SF) acquired by Capital Group from Brookfield Properties for ~$210M (~$150/SF). Capital Group already occupies ~324,000 SF in the building — owner-user acquisition of a distressed downtown tower.

---

## Pricing

Overall asking rent rose 1.3% QOQ and 0.7% YOY to $3.62/SF FSG. Class A rents up 1.2% QOQ and 1.6% YOY. Effective rents remain under pressure — landlords relying on higher concessions and plug-and-play space to attract tenants despite modest headline rent growth.

---

## Construction

One Q1 2026 delivery: Warner Bros. Ranch Lot Studios, 320,000 SF in Burbank — fully occupied by Warner Bros., adds no competitive vacancy. Development pipeline thin by historical standards.

---

## Outlook

Office market remains under pressure. Capital markets expected to regain some momentum. Tenants in Downtown LA shifting within CBD to Bunker Hill, often with significant footprint reductions. Law firms driving much of this activity.
