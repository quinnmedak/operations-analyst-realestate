# How Reframe Studios Is Rethinking The Future Of Production Space In LA

## Project Overview
Swift Real Estate Partners purchased a 7.3-acre industrial site in LA's Atwater Village in 2022 and converted it to Reframe Studios: two 23,000 SF soundstages + 100,000 SF of office/support space. Ceilings raised to 40+ feet from 22 feet, all interior columns removed, power upgraded from 2,000 to 10,000 amps. Previously occupied by Phillips Electronics for industrial use.

## LA Entertainment Industry and CRE
Los Angeles is the entertainment capital of the world but has undergone significant shifts: pandemic-induced downturn in film production and the 2023 writer/actor strikes disrupted traditional studio demand. Big-budget film production now competes with streaming content and social media creators (TikTok, Instagram, Twitch).

Studios now need to serve everything from major productions to 30-second content reels — driving demand for more flexible, multi-use production spaces.

## Adaptive Reuse Trend
Industrial-to-studio conversion is an emerging adaptive reuse trend in LA, distinct from the office-to-residential conversion story:
- Repurposes obsolete industrial buildings for entertainment/production use
- Serves both traditional TV/film and new media content creators
- Diversifies revenue through short-term event bookings alongside long-term tenants

## Tenant Landscape: Entertainment/Media in LA
LA entertainment and media industries are significant CRE occupiers but increasingly selectvie about space:
- Traditional TV/film studios (streamers, studios) seek purpose-built or high-spec production space
- New media / content creators need flexible, shorter-term arrangements
- The 2023 strikes accelerated restructuring of how entertainment companies use physical space

CBRE team (Doug Marlow, Juliana Sampson, Nicole Mihalka) brokered leasing for Reframe Studios.
