**Headline:** Signs of stabilization as net absorption turns positive for the first time since 2022

## Market Overview

The Los Angeles industrial market remained in a correction cycle but showed early signs of stabilization in Q1 2026. After two consecutive years of occupancy losses, including approximately 13.9 million sq. ft. of negative net absorption over the course of 2024, demand turned positive: Q1 2026 posted 934,000 sq. ft. of net absorption. Despite the growth in activity, vacancy continued to drift higher, reaching 5.4% in Q1 2026.

**Important caveat:** The quarter's positive net absorption was significantly influenced by the delivery and occupancy of Trader Joe's approximately 1.0 million sq. ft. distribution center in Palmdale. Without this single deal, net absorption would have been slightly negative.

## Availability Rate

The overall industrial availability rate closed Q1 2026 at 8.1%, up from 7.7% in Q4 2025 and 6.9% a year earlier. Over the past three years, availability has more than doubled from 4.0% in Q1 2023, reflecting a materially larger volume of space across both direct and sublease categories. Direct availability reached 6.9% in Q1 2026; sublease availability held at 1.2% (~10.5M SF).

**Submarket availability Q1 2026:**
- Greater San Fernando Valley: 6.8% total (5.7% direct, 1.1% sublease) — lowest
- San Gabriel Valley: 7.3% (5.9% direct, 1.5% sublease)
- Central Los Angeles: 8.0% (7.1% direct, 0.9% sublease)
- Mid-Counties: 9.1% (8.0% direct, 1.1% sublease)
- South Bay: 9.5% (7.9% direct, 1.6% sublease) — highest; highest sublease rate at 1.6%

## Asking Rent

Average direct asking rent fell to $1.21/SF NNN/mth in Q1 2026, down 1.6% QOQ from $1.23 and 8.3% YOY from $1.32. Since Q1 2023 ($1.74), the market has experienced a cumulative 30.5% decline.

**Submarket rents Q1 2026:**
- South Bay: $1.36/SF (highest)
- Greater San Fernando Valley: $1.34
- Mid-Counties: $1.26
- San Gabriel Valley: $1.22
- Central Los Angeles: $0.99 (lowest; $0.37 spread between high and low)

## Net Absorption

Q1 2026 posted 934,000 SF positive net absorption — first positive quarter since 2022, reversing from -1.9M SF in Q1 2025.

**By submarket:**
- South Bay: +643,000 SF
- Greater San Fernando Valley: +590,000 SF
- Central Los Angeles: +257,000 SF
- Mid-Counties: -320,000 SF
- San Gabriel Valley: -235,000 SF

## Construction Activity

- Q1 2026 deliveries: 1.6M SF (more than double the 0.7M SF delivered a year earlier)
- Full-year 2025 deliveries: 4.7M SF
- Pipeline: 4.5M SF under construction, down 19.1% QOQ from 5.6M SF in Q4 2025 and 34.5% YOY from 6.9M SF in Q1 2025 — developers pulling back

**Pipeline by submarket:**
- Greater San Fernando Valley: 1.2M SF (also recorded largest deliveries at 1.2M SF)
- San Gabriel Valley: 1.1M SF
- Central Los Angeles: 0.9M SF
- Mid-Counties: 0.8M SF
- South Bay: 0.6M SF

## Gross Activity (Leasing)

14.6M SF total gross activity in Q1 2026, up 27.3% QOQ and 4.4% YOY — highest in the prior three years.

**By submarket:**
- South Bay: 37.4% (5.5M SF)
- Central Los Angeles: 23.6% (3.5M SF)
- Greater San Fernando Valley: 13.7% (2.0M SF)
- Mid-Counties: 13.4% (2.0M SF)
- San Gabriel Valley: 11.9% (1.7M SF)

Driven by advanced manufacturing, logistics, food & beverage, and consumer products. Advanced manufacturing accounted for five of the top ten leases.

## Port Context

San Pedro Bay complex (Ports of LA and Long Beach) handled more than 20 million TEUs in 2025, +0.9% YOY. Port of LA: 10.2M TEUs (3rd busiest calendar year in 118 years). Port of Long Beach: 9.9M TEUs (all-time record).
