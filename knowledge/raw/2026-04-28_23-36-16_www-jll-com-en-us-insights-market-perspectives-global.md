## Office: Global Leasing at New Post-Pandemic High
Global office leasing rose 5% in 2025 to highest level since 2019. Groundbreakings at record lows in the U.S.; three-quarters of remaining pipeline already pre-leased. Dwindling availability of high-quality, central space is driving supply shortages and elevated rental growth for high-end space.

Future trends: Over 50% of organizations globally requiring 3-4 days in-office; workplace experience increasingly important. Companies moving into expansion mode, supporting continued gradual recovery in leasing through 2026.

## Logistics: Leasing Activity Rebounding
Logistics leasing demand continues to rebound from tariff uncertainty in H1 2025. Leasing increased in both North America and Europe in 2025. New supply decreased significantly from peak levels — will contribute to plateauing or falling vacancy.

Future trends: Demand expected to continue rising in most major markets through 2026, supported by regionalization of manufacturing/distribution, defense spending, and rising e-commerce.

## Retail: Resilient Demand
Retail spending expected to remain positive in most major markets through 2026. U.S. absorption rose in Q4, signaling ongoing recovery; store opening announcements outpace closures. Institutional capital surged back into retail.

## Living (Multifamily): Rising Capital
Global transaction volumes for living sector up 24% in 2025. U.S. continues to be dominant market at two-thirds of investment. Further growth expected in 2026 on improving debt availability.

## Capital Markets
Direct investment activity sustained momentum through Q4 2025; global volumes up 19% from 2024. Americas up 14% YoY in Q4. Cross-border investment up 25% YoY for full year 2025.
