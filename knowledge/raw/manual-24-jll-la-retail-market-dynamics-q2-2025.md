## Market Summary

Los Angeles' retail market remains resilient despite challenges. Net absorption stands at -879,255 SF YTD and -2.1M SF over 12 months, driven by consumer shifts and store closures. Yet vacancy holds at 5.8% with minimal construction (0.1% of inventory), limiting supply risk and creating opportunity for investors and retailers.

Leasing activity reveals a pronounced flight to quality, with Class A and new spaces commanding premium demand despite limited inventory. Significant transactions include Round1's 160,000 SF commitment at Stonewood Center, underscoring preference for high-traffic locations. The San Fernando and San Gabriel Valleys outperform with positive rent growth, contrasting with the metro's 1.0% decline, benefiting from suburban demographics and reduced dependence on international tourism.

Minimal construction activity (736,401 SF, mostly pre-leased) strengthens market fundamentals. Developers prioritize redevelopment and adaptive reuse over speculative projects, enhancing property values through strategic repositioning rather than new builds.

## Key Transaction

- **Round1 — 160,000 SF at Stonewood Center** — entertainment/experience tenant, high-traffic suburban location

## Submarket Performance

- **San Fernando Valley and San Gabriel Valley:** positive rent growth — outperforming the metro due to suburban demographics and reduced dependence on international tourism
- **Metro overall:** -1.0% rent decline

## Construction Pipeline

- 736,401 SF under construction, mostly pre-leased
- 0.1% of total inventory — effectively zero speculative supply risk
- Developer strategy: redevelopment and adaptive reuse over new builds

## Outlook

The Los Angeles retail market will likely continue to face headwinds through 2026, with rent growth trailing national averages as negative absorption persists. However, the limited new construction pipeline combined with ongoing demolition of obsolete space will help prevent a more dramatic rise in availability rates. Strategic opportunities exist for investors and retailers targeting premium space in resilient Los Angeles suburban submarkets.
