## Reflection

The JLL Business Intelligence Analyst posting is directly relevant to this course because it requires the exact skills we've built: SQL for descriptive and diagnostic analytics, extracting and transforming data from remote sources, and delivering insights to business stakeholders. In the first half of the course, we used SQL to uncover trends and explain root causes, then built presentation slides with clear takeaway titles to communicate findings across technical and non-technical audiences — directly matching the role's requirement to "communicate findings to technical and non-technical audiences effectively." In the second half, we built pipelines that connect to cloud databases on AWS, extract and aggregate data, and load it into a structured destination — mapping directly to the role's requirements around ETL processes, data warehousing, and familiarity with cloud platforms. To prove I can do this job, I would build a pipeline pulling commercial real estate market data from a public API (such as FRED, HUD, RentCast, or the Census Bureau) into Snowflake, transform it through a dbt star schema, and surface leasing and market insights in an interactive Streamlit dashboard — complemented by a knowledge base built from scraped industry research and market reports (such as JLL Research, CBRE, Bisnow, or GlobeSt). Through that dashboard and accompanying presentation slides, I will demonstrate the ability to draw descriptive insights (what is happening in the market?) and diagnostic insights (why is it happening?), translating them into actionable recommendations for business stakeholders. This project would transfer directly to roles like Data Analyst at a commercial real estate brokerage, Reporting Analyst at a property management firm, or BI Analyst at a PropTech company. Prior experience as a research intern at CBRE also satisfies the role's preferred qualification for industry-specific knowledge in commercial real estate.
